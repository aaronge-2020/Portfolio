About Me

Bridging AI innovation with clinical medicine to create equitable healthcare solutions

My work integrates artificial intelligence and clinical medicine to address systemic healthcare inequities, particularly for underserved populations. With 4 years of experience as an EMT responding to 911 emergencies through the COVID pandemic, I witnessed firsthand how socioeconomic barriers delay critical care and worsen outcomes.

At the National Cancer Institute, I developed privacy-preserving AI models to democratize access to precision medicine. I led FastImpute, an on-device machine learning tool for genotype imputation and breast cancer risk assessment, and created TMA-Grid, a CNN-powered web application for tissue microarray analysis now adopted by resource-limited labs.

As co-founder of Tensor Lab for Computational Medicine, I'm leading a highly selective summer research fellowship that mentors exceptional computer science students in applying their technical skills to high-impact medical challenges.
